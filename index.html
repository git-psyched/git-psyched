Why I Build

Noble version:

Medical education is expensive enough. Study resources shouldn't add to the financial burden. Every tool I create is free, has no ads, requires no login, and will always be open-source.

The Honest Version:

If I'm being real: I built this because I am an anxious person. I'm currently in that high-stakes cycle where resting feels like a security breach. There's this constant, nagging fear that if I stop moving, I'll lose everything I've worked for—or worse, the information I just spent 6 hours memorizing will simply evaporate.

I created these games as a "Productive Peace-Offering" for my own brain. When I've finished banging through 500+ Anki cards or a soul-crushing UWorld block, my body is wiped, but my mind is still redlining. I'm too fried for "Active Recall," but too caffeinated to actually "relax."

This is my way of reclaiming the "In-Between." It's Medical Pattern Recognition Training—at least, that's what I tell my therapist so I don't have to admit I've forgotten how to sit still. It's a low-stakes "Side Quest" designed for those few minutes of downtime before you head back into the grind.

I truly enjoy the art of studying, and I hope these tools help you appease your own "Guilt-Trip Simulator" while you work toward your goals.

Side effects may include improved board scores and a slightly less disappointed inner monologue.
